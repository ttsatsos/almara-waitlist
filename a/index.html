A peek inside Almara

Stop being the human reminder app.

Nagging trains kids to wait for the nag. Almara puts the daily check-in on them, so the question becomes "did I do my stuff?" instead of "is Mom about to yell?"

Bribes go up. This holds steady.

Five bucks becomes ten becomes "I'm not doing it for less than twenty." With almas, kids earn a steady reward currency and spend it on rewards you set. No escalation, no renegotiating at the kitchen table.

A reason to show up tomorrow.

A paper chart gives a sticker and dies. In Almara, a gentle avatar grows a little every day they check in, so the streak itself becomes the thing they don't want to break.

How it works

  1. Set the stuff and the rewards. Add their chores, routines, and homework, then pick the rewards they can cash almas in for. You stay in control of both.
  2. Kid checks in, you approve. They mark what they did each day. You give the nod. Their avatar grows and the almas land.
  3. They spend, you stop nagging. Kids trade almas for the rewards you chose. The motivation lives in the app now, not in your voice.
